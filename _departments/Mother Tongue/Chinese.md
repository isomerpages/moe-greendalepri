<h2><strong>Chinese</strong></h2>
<h4><strong>Philosophy</strong></h4>
<p>The Mother Tongue Language Department, guided by the core philosophy of
"Joy of Learning", is committed to nurturing students into well-rounded
individuals who "Love the Language, Love the Culture, and are Future-Ready".</p>
<p>To cultivate a love for the language, our curriculum takes a comprehensive
approach to developing core skills. Building on a strong foundation in
listening, speaking, and oral interaction, we further strengthen students'
abilities in reading, writing, and written communication. Through creating
an immersive learning environment, teachers actively broaden the spaces
in which students can use the language, enhancing its practical value in
everyday life so that it truly becomes a living language for our students.</p>
<p>In upholding the goal of nurturing a love for culture, the school places
great emphasis on character development. Guided by the "21st Century Competencies
Core Values", cultural appreciation is woven into daily instruction, carefully
cultivating in students’ strong moral character and sound values.</p>
<p>To shape a generation of Future-Ready Learners, teachers aspire to skillfully
integrate diverse pedagogical approaches with information technology in
the classroom to spark students' enthusiasm for learning. By creating authentic
and interactive learning contexts, we provide personalized learning experiences
that empower students with the essential competencies to navigate and explore
their own futures.</p>
<h4>Chinese Language Key Programmes (主要活动)</h4>
<p><strong><u>Mother Tongue Fortnight &amp; Language and Cultural Camp</u></strong>
</p>
<p>Our school's annual "Mother Tongue Fortnight" is an important platform
for putting the "Joy of Learning" philosophy into practice. To guide students
in developing a love for culture, we organise a series of engaging and
enjoyable learning activities that allow students to experience Chinese
martial arts and traditional games first-hand, discovering the essence
of Chinese culture in a fun and relaxed atmosphere. Through these immersive
cultural experiences, the school creates authentic contexts for students
to engage in oral interaction in Mandarin, encouraging them to listen and
speak more during the activities, and naturally deepening their love for
their mother tongue.</p>
<p>To nurture future-ready learners who have a love for the language, Primary
5 students challenge themselves during the Language &amp; Cultural Camp
by learning how to write Chinese scripts and produce short digital videos,
as well as actively participating in competitions. This signature programme
keeps Chinese Language learning in step with the times — significantly
strengthening students' oral expression and written communication skills,
while achieving a seamless integration of traditional culture and future-ready
digital competencies.</p>

<p><strong>Reading Programme</strong>
</p>
<p>Our school's Reading Programme aims to cultivate a vibrant culture of
joyful learning across the school through diverse reading experiences.
For Primary 1 to 3, building on the Ministry of Education's SOAR reading
programme, we have created class libraries where teachers read picture
books together with students and facilitate interactive discussions. Primary
4 students engage in the guided reading of the classic novel "Journey to
the West", immersing themselves in the beauty of culture through literary
classics, before bringing the experience to life through a related theatre
performance funded by the Cultural Performance Exposure Scheme (CPES).</p>
<p>To promote the development of communication skills under the enhanced
21st Century Core Competencies (E21CC), reading materials for Primary 5
and 6 shifts fully towards newspaper reading activities centered on authentic
materials. Through ZBschools, an online Chinese news platform, students
select and read current affairs articles closely connected to everyday
life, broadening their global perspectives and deepening their learning
through written reflections.</p>
