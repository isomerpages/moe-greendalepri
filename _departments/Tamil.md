<h2><strong>Tamil</strong></h2>
<h4><strong>Philosophy</strong></h4>
<p>The Greendale Tamil Unit is committed to fostering a love for the Tamil
Language and culture through innovative and inclusive teaching. Aligned
with the MOE Tamil Curriculum, the department develops students' proficiency
in the respective language skills while nurturing confident and proud users
of Tamil. Through learning journeys, departmental programmes, and Mother
Tongue Fortnight activities, students build a strong sense of identity,
cultural appreciation, and belonging that will stay with them throughout
their lives.</p>
<p>Teachers integrate EdTech to create engaging, interactive, and personalised
learning experiences. Through platforms such as the Student Learning Space
(SLS) and multimedia tools, students develop their Tamil language skills
while benefiting from collaborative and self-paced learning opportunities
that cater to their individual needs.</p>
<p>In the lower primary classrooms, students learn through fun, hands-on
activities such as songs, storytelling, role-play, picture matching, playdough
letter formation, and magnetic word-building. These engaging experiences
build vocabulary, pronunciation, communication skills, confidence, and
a love for the Tamil language and culture.</p>
<p>As students progress to the middle and upper primary levels, they are
equipped with explicit annotation strategies to strengthen comprehension
and cloze skills. A progressive writing programme, supported by mind maps
and constructive feedback, develops their composition writing skills through
a structured and gradual approach.</p>

<h4>Tamil Language Key Programmes</h4>
<p><strong>Mother Tongue Fortnight</strong>
</p>
<p>As part of Mother Tongue Language Fortnight, students participate in a
variety of engaging activities that deepen their appreciation of the Tamil
Language, culture, and traditions. They explore traditional Indian sports
such as Kabaddi and Kho Kho. They also take part in interactive cultural
booth activities, including arranging traditional South Indian dishes on
a banana leaf. Students enjoy heritage games such as Pambaram, Aadu Puli
Aatam, and Pallanguzhi. These meaningful, hands-on experiences make learning
enjoyable while fostering cultural awareness, appreciation, and pride.</p>

<p><strong>EXTERNAL TAMIL LANGUAGE COMPETITIONS</strong>
</p>
<p>Our Primary 4 and 5 students regularly participate in prestigious national
platforms, including the National Primary School Tamil Spelling Bee Competition
by Mediacorp. These events offer invaluable opportunities for students
to showcase their linguistic talents, build confidence, and apply their
learning beyond the classroom.</p>

<p><strong>DEEPAVALI CELEBRATIONS</strong>
</p>
<p>Our Deepavali celebration provide students with meaningful opportunities
to learn about the festival and its traditions.</p>
<p>During recess, students participate in hands-on booth activities such
as Kolam decorating and Kolam design cutting. These interactive experiences
deepen students' understanding and appreciation of Deepavali in an enjoyable
and memorable way.</p>
